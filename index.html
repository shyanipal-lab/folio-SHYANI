How I work

Process, not magic.
(Although sometimes it feels like magic. I take full credit.)

01
Understand the problem
I start by talking to users and stakeholders — not just to gather data, but because listening first is how you avoid designing solutions to the wrong problem. (Hard lesson. Learned it the fun way.)
02
Frame with clarity
A well-framed problem is half the solution. I write clear problem statements, define success metrics, and align the team before any pixels move. Yes, before.
03
Explore with intent
I explore multiple directions with lo-fi flows, test assumptions early, and cut ruthlessly based on evidence — not aesthetics, opinion, or whoever spoke last in the standup.
04
Ship & learn
I treat the first release as a hypothesis. I track metrics, synthesise feedback, and push for the next iteration. Design doesn't stop at handoff — unlike my attention span during all-hands meetings.

* No designers were harmed in the making of these processes. Several sticky notes were.

About me

I help teams make better decisions through design.
(I also have opinions about font sizes. Everyone knows.)

I'm Shyani — a product designer with 4+ years of experience across enterprise mobility, fintech, and consumer apps. I'm currently designing in-car and connected experiences at Mercedes-Benz R&D in Bengaluru, where my commute and my job description are ironically disconnected.

My approach is commercially aware and pragmatic. I work closely with PMs and engineers, push back when needed, and ship work I'm proud of. I'm not precious about ideas — I care about outcomes. (My stakeholders appreciate this. Mostly.)
